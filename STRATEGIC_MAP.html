4. Are you building toward the goal?

Yes — but you're further from "portfolio throughput" than you think, and closer to "licensable engine" than you think.

The math + policy + skills + harness stack is genuinely licensable-grade. A bank's credit committee could drop a deal in your engine today and get a defensible BDSCR, rate-shock, and policy check with audit trail. That's rare. Most loan software on the market is spreadsheet wrappers. You have a deterministic engine with regression coverage. That's the product.

What's NOT licensable yet is the operator experience. No bank is going to buy a "run one skill at a time from Claude chat" product. They need a state machine, an inbox, a portfolio view, and clean multi-tenant data isolation. Those don't exist.

Roughly: engine 90% done, operator surface 10% done. The remaining work is almost entirely operator surface.

5. Most effective next step

You explicitly said: "I have struggled with managing the portfolio" and "I want to automate contracts like this where I am only approving the initial spreads, underwriting, memo changes, final approval."

That is the Portfolio State Machine + Decision Inbox. One artifact. Four things it does:

  1. A JSON-backed portfolio.json that tracks every active deal: stage, last action, next action, assigned decision, days-in-stage.
  2. A stage model: intake → spreads_ready → uw_complete → memo_draft → memo_final → committee → decision → closing → purge. Each stage ends at exactly one of your 4 decision points.
  3. A single HTML dashboard (like this one) regenerated on command. Opens in a browser from your desktop. Shows "needs you today" at the top, full portfolio below.
  4. A promote_deal skill that advances state when you approve, logs the decision + timestamp, and fires the next skill in the chain automatically.

Why this is the highest-leverage move:

  • Every existing skill plugs in unchanged. Zero rewrites.
  • It solves the exact pain you named.
  • It's the 4-decision-point UX you described, built natively.
  • Desktop-efficient by design: one HTML file, no server, no app install.
  • It's the same thing a CDFI or bank client would pay for — you'll eat your own cooking on Swift Capital first, then sell it.
  • It converts 20 isolated skills into 1 managed operation. This is the loom.
