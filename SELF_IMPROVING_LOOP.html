4. Company-first posture — stop leaking into client infrastructure

CURRENT LEAK

  • Swift Capital work routes through keith@swiftcapitaloptions.com (client's Google Workspace)
  • Deal documents live in CIF's ShareFile — you download copies but the system of record is theirs
  • Committee emails route through client-owned threads
  • CIF procedures and templates are stored in Swift Capital Underwriting/ — outside your build tree

Risk: If the contract ends, you lose access to most of the workflow history. The IP you're building leaks into their stack.

COMPANY-FIRST FIX

  • All outbound deliverables for Swift route through keith@sbalendingnetwork.com, CC'ing Swift addresses — not FROM them
  • Every Swift deal mirrored into Agentic_Overhaul/portfolio/deals/{deal_id}/ on your drive + GitHub
  • CIF procedures YAML (cif_procedures.yaml) lives in YOUR repo — it's your IP, not theirs
  • Monthly CDFI presentations generated from YOUR portfolio.json, delivered as PDFs branded SBA Lending Network / Swift Capital Services
  • ShareFile MCP when built: pulls docs INTO your pipeline, never pushes into theirs

Benefit: Contract-portable. Licensable. When you sell this to the next CDFI, nothing has to move.

6. Stance — the sequence, in order

Step 1 next: Portfolio State Machine + Decision Inbox. Built inside Agentic_Overhaul/portfolio/. One session.

Why this first: it's the visibility you said you're missing ("I'm building but cannot see what is going on"). Without it, everything else is more code you can't see. With it, you have a single HTML you open from your desktop showing every deal, every stage, every decision waiting on you.

Step 2 right after: Scheduled Audit Loop. Nightly harness run + weekly memory consolidation + monthly skill-metric digest. One hour of setup. Converts you from "I run the harness when I remember" to "the harness runs itself and tells me when something broke."

Step 3 after that: CDFI Presentation Kit. Monthly report template generated from your portfolio.json. Branded as SBA Lending Network. Turns your CIF work into sales material for the next CDFI. Feeds the licensing goal.

Steps 4-7 wait. Don't start MCP connectors, metrics dashboards, or multi-tenant packaging until 1-3 are live and you've run them against one real month of Swift Capital work. If those three ship and you can't tell me they saved you time, something's wrong with the design and I'll rebuild before adding surface area.

Hard rule: every session from here ends with a 16/16 green harness. No exceptions. That's the "well-oiled machine" discipline.
